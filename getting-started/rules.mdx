DirtShop SMP is a community built on mutual respect, fair play, and good vibes. To keep it that way, every player — new or veteran — is expected to follow the rules outlined on this page. These guidelines exist to protect you and everyone else on the server, so please read them in full before you start playing.

<Warning>
  **Ignorance of the rules is not an excuse.** By joining DirtShop SMP, you agree to abide by all of the rules listed here. "I didn't know" will not be accepted as a defense when a punishment is issued.
</Warning>

## General Conduct

The golden rule on DirtShop SMP is simple: **treat every player the way you want to be treated.**

- Respect all players regardless of their skill level, playstyle, or background.
- Harassment, bullying, targeted insults, and personal attacks are strictly prohibited.
- Hate speech, slurs, and discrimination of any kind — whether based on race, gender, religion, nationality, sexuality, or any other characteristic — will result in an immediate and severe punishment.
- Keep the overall atmosphere of the server family-friendly. If you wouldn't say it in front of a ten-year-old, think twice before saying it here.

## Griefing & Stealing

Protecting players' hard work is one of our top priorities.

- **Griefing is not allowed.** Do not destroy, damage, or alter another player's builds — this includes placing blocks on or around them to grief, filling areas with lava or water, or any action intended to damage someone else's work.
- **Stealing is not allowed.** Do not take items from another player's chests, farms, or storage without explicit permission.
- **Land claiming protects your builds.** Use the claim system (see [First Steps](/getting-started/first-steps)) to claim the land around your builds. Builds left unclaimed are harder to protect after the fact, so claim early.
- Griefing or stealing in unclaimed areas is still a punishable offense — "it wasn't claimed" is not a loophole.

## Cheating & Hacks

DirtShop SMP is a fair-play server. Any advantage gained through external software or game exploits undermines the experience for everyone.

**The following are strictly prohibited:**
- Hacked clients or clients with built-in cheats (speed hacks, fly hacks, kill aura, etc.)
- X-ray texture packs or mods that reveal ores or cave systems through solid blocks
- Auto-clickers, macros, or any automation that allows you to play while AFK in a way that gives an unfair advantage
- Exploiting duplication glitches, item bugs, or any unintended game mechanics for personal gain

**The following are explicitly allowed:**
- **OptiFine** — performance and visual enhancement mod
- **Minimaps** — are permitted provided cave mapping and player radar features are disabled
- **Inventory management mods** (e.g., inventory sorters) that don't automate gameplay

If you're unsure whether a specific mod or client is allowed, ask a staff member before using it — it's always better to check first.

## Chat Rules

Chat is how the community connects, so keeping it clean and respectful is everyone's responsibility.

- **No spam.** Do not send the same message repeatedly or flood chat with rapid-fire messages.
- **No excessive caps.** THE OCCASIONAL EMPHASIS IS FINE, BUT TYPING ENTIRE SENTENCES IN CAPS IS NOT.
- **No advertising.** Do not promote other Minecraft servers, Discord servers, YouTube channels, or external websites in chat. Sharing personal social media with friends in private messages is fine.
- **No sharing personal information.** Do not share your own — or anyone else's — real name, address, phone number, or other identifying details in public chat.
- Keep conversations in the appropriate channels. Use `/msg` for private conversations and keep global chat friendly and on-topic.

## Punishment System

Staff apply punishments progressively, starting with lighter actions and escalating for repeat or severe offenses. Serious violations (hate speech, doxxing, cheating) may skip directly to a higher tier.

<Accordion title="Verbal Warning">
  **What it is:** A staff member will reach out in chat or via private message to inform you that your behavior is in violation of the rules.

  **What to do:** Acknowledge the warning and correct your behavior. A verbal warning is a courtesy — it will not always be given before a more serious punishment for clear or severe violations.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Mute">
  **What it is:** Your ability to send messages in public chat is temporarily removed. The duration depends on the severity and frequency of the offense — typically ranging from 30 minutes to 24 hours.

  **What to do:** Wait out the mute and reflect on what led to it. Repeated mutes will escalate to a temporary ban.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Temporary Ban">
  **What it is:** You are removed from the server and prevented from rejoining for a set period of time. Durations vary from 24 hours to 30 days depending on the infraction.

  **What to do:** If you believe the ban was issued in error, you can appeal through the [DirtShop SMP Discord](/community/discord). Follow the appeal process outlined in the `#ban-appeals` channel.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Permanent Ban">
  **What it is:** Your account is permanently barred from DirtShop SMP. This is reserved for the most severe violations — hate speech, cheating, repeated major offenses, or failure to comply after a temporary ban.

  **What to do:** Permanent bans may be appealed on a case-by-case basis through the Discord. Appeals are reviewed by senior staff and are not guaranteed to result in an unban.
</Accordion>

## Useful Commands

A few commands make it easy to stay informed and get help without ever leaving the game.

| Command | What it does |
|---|---|
| `/rules` | Displays the full server rule list in-game so you can review them at any time. |
| `/report <player>` | Opens a report form to flag a player to online staff. Replace `<player>` with their in-game username. |
| `/helpop <message>` | Sends a private message to all online staff members. Use this when you need immediate help and there's no staff visible in chat. |

**Examples:**

```
/rules
/report Steve123
/helpop Someone is griefing my base near coords 200, 64, -400
```

<Info>
  `/report` and `/helpop` both go directly to staff — use `/report` for filing a record of a violation and `/helpop` when you need an urgent real-time response.
</Info>

## Reporting Players

If you witness a rule violation, don't take matters into your own hands — report it to staff. Use `/report <player>` in-game to flag the incident, and include as much detail as you can. See the [Reporting Players](/community/reporting) page for step-by-step instructions on how to submit a report, what evidence to gather, and what to expect after a report is filed.
