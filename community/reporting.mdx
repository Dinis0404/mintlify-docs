Keeping DirtShop SMP a fair and enjoyable place depends on players and staff working together. If you witness someone breaking the rules — whether it's griefing, hacking, harassment, or cheating — you have the tools to report it quickly. And if you've received a punishment you believe was incorrect, you have a clear path to appeal. This page covers both processes in full.

---

## Reporting a Player

### Your Reporting Options

There are three ways to report a player depending on the situation and whether staff are currently online:

- **In-game report** — use `/report <player> <reason>` to instantly flag a player and alert all online staff members. This is the fastest option and should be your first step when staff are active.
- **Staff message** — use `/helpop <message>` to send a private message directly to all online staff. Use this to provide extra context after filing a `/report`, or when you need urgent help that doesn't fit a standard report format.
- **Discord ticket** — if no staff are online or if you don't receive a response in-game, open a ticket in the **#help-desk** channel on Discord. This method is also better suited for complex reports that require sharing screenshots or video evidence.

### What to Include in Your Report

A strong report makes it much easier for staff to investigate and act. Always try to include:

- The **exact in-game username** of the player you are reporting
- A clear **description of what happened** and which rule was broken
- **Screenshots or a screen recording** — press `F2` in Minecraft to take a screenshot, or use software like OBS or your system's built-in screen recorder for video
- **Coordinates** (press `F3` to view them) if the incident involves griefing, an illegal build, or a location-specific exploit

### Step-by-Step Reporting Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Gather Your Evidence">
    Before doing anything else, take screenshots or start a screen recording. Press `F2` to capture a screenshot in Minecraft — files are saved to your `.minecraft/screenshots` folder. If the incident is ongoing, a short video clip is far more compelling than a static screenshot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit an In-Game Report">
    Run `/report <player> <reason>` in chat, replacing `<player>` with the offending player's username and `<reason>` with a brief description of the rule they broke. Example: `/report Steve123 Using a hacked client / flying in survival`. This immediately alerts all online staff.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow Up With /helpop if Needed">
    If you need to give staff additional context right away, run `/helpop <message>` to send them a direct private message. For example: `/helpop Following up on my report — Steve123 is still flying near /warp market`. Staff see these messages instantly, making `/helpop` ideal for urgent situations while an investigation is already underway.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a Discord Ticket if Needed">
    If no staff are online or you don't receive an acknowledgment within a few minutes, head to **#help-desk** in the Discord server and open a support ticket. Attach your evidence directly in the ticket and provide the same details listed above. Staff monitor tickets even when offline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for Staff to Investigate">
    Once your report is submitted, staff will review the evidence and investigate the accused player's logs and activity. You can expect a response or resolution **within 24 hours**. Avoid confronting the reported player in chat while the investigation is open.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Filing a false report against an innocent player is itself a punishable offense. Deliberately wasting staff time or attempting to get someone falsely banned can result in a mute or ban on your own account. Only submit reports when you have genuine evidence of a rule violation.
</Warning>

## Appealing a Punishment

### Where to Appeal

All punishment appeals — whether for a mute, temporary ban, or permanent ban — are handled through the **#ban-appeals** channel in the DirtShop SMP Discord. Open a ticket there and a senior staff member will be assigned to review your case.

### What to Include in Your Appeal

To give your appeal the best chance of success, include the following in your ticket:

- Your **exact Minecraft username**
- The **type of punishment** you received (mute, temporary ban, permanent ban) and its duration
- The **reason given** when the punishment was issued
- Your explanation of **why you believe the punishment was incorrect**, excessive, or should be reduced — be honest and specific; vague appeals are harder to act on
- Any **evidence** that supports your case (screenshots, timestamps, context)

### The Appeals Process

Once you open your ticket, a senior staff member will review your appeal and the original punishment logs. You can expect a decision within **48 hours**. The reviewing staff member may ask you follow-up questions in the ticket — respond promptly to avoid delays.

All appeal decisions made by senior staff are **final**. If your appeal is denied, the punishment will stand and the ticket will be closed. Repeatedly reopening tickets for the same punishment or harassing staff over an appeal decision may result in additional consequences.

<Note>
  Temporary punishments of **24 hours or less** are generally not worth appealing — by the time a staff member reviews your ticket, the punishment will have already expired. Save your appeal for longer bans or situations where you genuinely believe an error was made.
</Note>
