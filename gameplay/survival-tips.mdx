Jumping into DirtShop SMP for the first time — or even coming back after a break — can feel overwhelming. The server has its own systems, customs, and pitfalls that vanilla Minecraft doesn't prepare you for. This guide walks you through the most important habits to build early, the tools the server gives you to succeed, and the mistakes that catch new players off guard.

---

## Protect Your Build First

Before you do anything else, **claim your land**. On DirtShop SMP, the Overworld is a permanent world where player builds persist indefinitely — but protection only applies to land you've actively claimed. Unclaimed land has no server-side protection, meaning other players can break blocks, take items from chests, and modify your builds without any rule being broken.

- Claim land using a **golden shovel**. Right-click two opposite corners of the area you want to protect.
- You start with a set number of claim blocks, and you earn more over time by playing on the server.
- Expand your claim as your base grows — don't leave new additions unprotected overnight.
- Use `/claimlist` to see all your active claims and their sizes.

<Warning>
  The server does not roll back grief on unclaimed land. If you haven't claimed it, it isn't protected — no exceptions. Always claim before you log off.
</Warning>

## Use the Resource World

One of the most important habits you can build on DirtShop SMP is **never mining or deforesting in the main Overworld**. The Overworld is a shared permanent world, and strip mines, quarries, and deforested hillsides create lasting visual damage for the entire community.

Instead, use the **Resource World**, a dedicated dimension that exists purely for gathering raw materials. It's reset monthly, so it always has fresh terrain, full ore veins, and untouched forests.

- Type `/warp resource` to teleport there instantly.
- Mine freely — dig quarries, clear entire forests, drain oceans. It's all fair game.
- Bring everything you collect back to the Overworld before the monthly reset, or it's gone.

See the [World Overview](/gameplay/world-overview) page for more details on the Resource World reset schedule.

## Nether Travel

The Nether is your best friend for fast Overworld travel, thanks to the **1:8 distance ratio** — one block of travel in the Nether equals eight blocks in the Overworld. This makes Nether portals an incredibly powerful tool for reaching your base quickly from spawn.

- **Build your Nether portal close to spawn** for fast access. A portal 500 blocks from spawn in the Nether connects to a point 4,000 blocks away in the Overworld.
- To find the correct Nether coordinates for a specific Overworld destination, divide your Overworld X and Z coordinates by 8.
- Use the **Nether highway at y=120** to travel quickly along the cardinal directions. Grab a boat and use the ice roads for near-instant long-distance travel.
- Always light your Nether tunnel well and bring extra food — Ghasts and Piglins don't care that you're in a hurry.

## Farming for Income

One of the best ways to build up your coin balance early in the game is through **automated farms**. These farms run passively and produce large quantities of sellable items without much active effort once they're set up.

The best early-game farm options are:

| Farm Type | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Wheat** | Simple to automate with water; sells consistently |
| **Sugarcane** | Fast growth; high sell volume; great for paper |
| **Cactus** | Fully passive; no water needed; good bulk coin earner |

Once you have a solid coin base, consider expanding into mob farms for XP and drops like bones, arrows, and rotten flesh — or into more specialized farms for items that are in high demand in the player market.

Head to `/warp shop` to check current server buy prices before deciding what to farm.

## Enchanting & Gear Progression

DirtShop SMP follows **standard vanilla gear and enchanting progression**. There are no custom enchants or pay-to-win gear upgrades — everything you earn, you earn through normal Minecraft gameplay.

- Progress through the standard diamond → Netherite pipeline for armor and tools.
- Build or use an **enchanting table** with bookshelves (15 bookshelves for max level enchants).
- Pre-built enchanting tables and a community **library** are available at `/spawn` — great for early-game players who haven't set up their own yet.
- Combine books using an **anvil** to get the exact enchants you want on a piece of gear.
- Mending is one of the most valuable enchants on the server — prioritize getting it on your best tools and armor early.

## Avoid Common Mistakes

<Accordion title="I got griefed — what do I do?">
  If your base was griefed, the most likely cause is that your land was **not claimed**. Unfortunately, the server cannot roll back damage to unclaimed land.

  Going forward, protect yourself by claiming your land with a **golden shovel**:
  1. Hold the golden shovel in your hand.
  2. Right-click one corner of the area you want to claim.
  3. Right-click the opposite corner diagonally.
  4. Your land is now claimed and protected.

  If you believe your claimed land was griefed, contact a staff member immediately with as much detail as possible.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I died and lost my items — can I get them back?">
  DirtShop SMP does **not offer item recovery** for deaths. There is no rollback, no staff intervention, and no death chest plugin that holds your items indefinitely.

  To protect your most valuable items:
  - Store gear you can't afford to lose in an **ender chest** — its contents are tied to your player, not the block, and survive your death.
  - Never carry your best tools and armor into high-risk situations (PvP zones, end-game dungeons) without a backup set.
  - Keep a second set of essentials (food, torches, basic tools) in your base for after a bad death.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I can't find my base — how do I get back?">
  If you can't find your way back to your base, you most likely haven't set a home yet. On DirtShop SMP, you can save your location using the `/sethome` command.

  - Stand at your base and type `/sethome base` to save it as a named home called "base".
  - Teleport back any time with `/home base`.
  - You can save multiple homes (depending on your rank) — set one at your base, one at your farm, and one near the resource world portal.

  If you've already set a home but forgotten where it is relative to the map, try `/home base` to warp directly there.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I can't build or the land is already claimed">
  If you're trying to build somewhere and getting blocked, the land you've chosen is **already claimed by another player**. This is common close to spawn or in popular biomes.

  - Travel further from spawn (at least 2,000–3,000 blocks) to find unclaimed wilderness.
  - Use `/map` or check the server's live map (if available) to see claimed zones.
  - Ask in chat if you're unsure — the community is generally happy to help direct new players to open land.
</Accordion>
