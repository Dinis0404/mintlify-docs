The DirtShop SMP economy is built entirely around player activity. There's no admin-generated wealth, no pay-to-win shortcuts, and no magic money printer — every coin in circulation was earned by a player doing something useful. Whether you're a fresh spawn looking to scrape together your first few hundred coins or a seasoned trader managing a profitable shop empire, understanding how the economy works will help you make the most of your time on the server.

---

## Currency

The server's currency is called **coins**. You earn them, spend them, and trade them with other players for goods and services. Every transaction on the server runs through coins.

You can check your current balance at any time using:

- `/money` — displays your current coin balance
- `/bal` — shorthand alias for the same command

Your balance persists between sessions, so there's no pressure to spend everything before you log off.

## Earning Coins

There are several reliable ways to build your coin balance on DirtShop SMP. The best players use a mix of all of them.

### Selling to the Server Shop

The server shop at `/warp shop` buys a wide range of items at fixed prices. It's the most reliable way to turn raw resources into coins, especially early in the game when you don't have a player shop set up yet.

- Visit `/warp shop` and interact with the shop interface.
- Check which items have good buy rates before deciding what to farm.
- Common high-value items include common ores, farm crops, mob drops, and building materials.

### Setting Up a Player Chest Shop

Player chest shops let you sell items directly to other players at prices you set yourself. A well-stocked shop in the player market at `/warp market` can become a major passive income source.

See the [Shops](/gameplay/shops) guide for full instructions on setting up a chest shop.

### Completing Jobs

If the server is running a jobs plugin, you can earn coins passively just by doing normal Minecraft activities like mining, farming, fishing, and building.

- Browse available jobs with `/jobs browse`.
- Join a job with `/jobs join <jobname>`.
- You'll earn coins automatically as you perform actions related to your chosen job.
- You can typically hold multiple jobs at once — experiment to find the combination that matches how you play.

### Voting for the Server

Voting is one of the easiest and most consistent ways to earn bonus coins. Each time you vote for DirtShop SMP on a server listing site, you receive a coin reward and sometimes bonus items.

- Type `/vote` in-game to see all available voting links.
- Voting once per day on each site adds up quickly over time.
- Vote streaks may award bonus rewards — check the vote reward board at spawn for current bonuses.

## Spending Coins

Coins flow back out of your balance through several channels:

- **Player Shops** — buy items from chest shops run by other players. Browse active shops with `/shops`.
- **Server Shop** — buy items at fixed prices at `/warp shop`. Some items are only available here.
- **Rank Upgrades** — certain playtime or donor rank upgrades may require coins through in-game purchase.
- **Shop Plot Rentals** — renting a stall at `/warp market` costs a recurring coin fee via `/plotshop buy`.
- **Player Trades** — direct player-to-player coin transfers with `/pay <player> <amount>`.

## Leaderboard

Want to see how you stack up against the wealthiest players on the server? Use `/baltop` to view a ranked list of players by coin balance. It's a useful tool for spotting successful traders and shop owners — and figuring out what they're doing that you aren't yet.

---

## Anti-Inflation Tips

Because the economy is player-driven, pricing is set by supply and demand. Underselling your items doesn't just hurt your income — it drives down market prices for everyone. Follow these habits to protect your earnings and the broader economy:

- **Check `/warp market` prices first.** Before you list anything in your shop, see what other players are charging. Don't drastically undercut the going rate.
- **Don't flood the market.** If you have a massive surplus of one item, consider selling it in smaller batches over time rather than dumping it all at once.
- **Specialize where possible.** Being the best-stocked shop for one item category is more profitable than a mediocre shop across all categories.
- **Monitor demand.** Items that are easy to farm but hard to find in player shops command a premium. Identify gaps and fill them.

<Note>
  The DirtShop SMP economy is entirely player-driven. The server does not inject coins into the economy beyond fixed vote rewards and job payouts — everything else flows from player-to-player trade and server shop transactions. This means the health of the economy depends on every player pricing fairly, trading actively, and participating in the market.
</Note>
