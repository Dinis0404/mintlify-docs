Commerce is one of the most rewarding parts of DirtShop SMP. The server gives you everything you need to run a thriving business — from fixed-price server shops to fully customizable player chest shops and a community market with dedicated stalls. Whether you're looking to offload excess farm produce or build a proper storefront, this guide walks you through every option available.

---

## Server Shop

The quickest way to sell items on DirtShop SMP is through the **server shop**, accessible at `/warp shop`.

- The server shop offers **fixed prices** for a wide range of items — no haggling, no waiting for a buyer.
- It's a reliable baseline for selling common resources like crops, ores, and mob drops.
- Prices at the server shop are set by the server and do not fluctuate. If you can sell the same item to a player shop for more, do that instead.
- You can also **buy** certain items from the server shop at fixed prices, which is useful when you need something in a hurry.

## Player Chest Shops

Player chest shops let you sell items directly to other players at prices you control. Once set up, your shop runs passively — players can buy and sell from it even while you're offline.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Place a chest">
    Find a location for your shop — either at your base, in a dedicated shop building, or in a rented plot at `/warp market`. Place a standard chest where you want the shop to be.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place a sign on the chest">
    Right-click or place a sign directly on the front face of the chest (or on a block adjacent to it, depending on the plugin). This sign will define your shop's item, price, and type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in Line 1 — your username">
    On the first line of the sign, type your in-game username. Some shop plugins allow you to leave this blank and will fill it in automatically. Check with staff or in-game instructions if you're unsure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in Line 2 — quantity">
    On the second line, enter the quantity of items per transaction. For example, type `10` to sell items in stacks of 10 at a time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in Line 3 — buy and sell prices">
    On the third line, set your pricing using the format `B <buy price> : S <sell price>`. For example:
    - `B 100 : S 50` means players can **buy** the item from you for 100 coins, or **sell** the item to you for 50 coins.
    - Set only a buy price (`B 100`) if you only want to sell, not buy.
    - Set only a sell price (`S 50`) if you only want to buy items from players.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in Line 4 — item name">
    On the fourth line, enter the item's in-game ID or name in all caps. For example: `DIAMOND`, `WHEAT`, `OAK_LOG`, `IRON_INGOT`. Use the standard Minecraft item ID format.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stock the chest">
    Once the sign is placed and configured, open the chest and fill it with the item you're selling. Your shop is now live. Customers who right-click the sign will be able to buy or sell the listed item automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If the chest is empty, players will not be able to buy from your shop. Make sure to restock regularly, especially if you're selling high-demand items. You'll receive a coin payment automatically when a purchase is made.
</Note>

## Player Market

The **Player Market** at `/warp market` is a community-built shopping area with dedicated pre-built shop plots available to rent. It's the best place to set up a public-facing shop because of the built-in foot traffic from other players browsing the area.

- **Access:** Type `/warp market` to teleport to the market.
- **Renting a Stall:** Use `/plotshop buy` while standing in an available plot to rent it. Plots require a recurring coin payment to maintain.
- **Shop Setup:** Once you've rented a plot, set up your chest shops inside it following the same steps above.
- **Visibility:** Shops in the market are seen by every player who visits, giving you far more exposure than a shop hidden in the wilderness.

## Browsing Shops

You don't have to wander the map to find good deals. DirtShop SMP includes a shop directory you can access directly in-game.

- `/shops` — opens a list of active player shops on the server. You can browse by item, price, or player.
- `/warp market` — physically visit the player market to browse stalls in person.

Check both regularly — the `/shops` list may include shops outside the market area that have better prices on what you need.

## Quick-Sell Commands

If you've just returned from a mining or farming run and want to offload items fast, DirtShop SMP has a set of quick-sell commands that let you sell directly without navigating the shop interface manually.

- `/shop` — opens the server shop GUI from anywhere, so you can buy or sell items without walking to `/warp shop`.
- `/sell hand` — instantly sells the item stack you're currently holding to the server shop at the fixed server price.
- `/sellall` — sells every item in your inventory that the server shop accepts, all in one command. Perfect for clearing out a full mining haul in seconds.

<Tip>
  Use `/sellall` right after a Resource World mining session to convert your inventory into coins without visiting the market. It's the fastest way to liquidate raw materials when you just want to get back to building.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Competitive pricing and visibility are everything.** Before listing your items, use `/shops` and walk through `/warp market` to see what others are charging. Aim to price slightly better than the competition — even a few coins difference can make your shop the go-to option. Once your shop is open, **advertise it in the Discord #marketplace channel** with your warp or location, what you sell, and your prices. Players actively look there for the best deals, and a single post can drive a wave of new customers.
</Tip>
