DirtShop SMP is committed to transparent, consistent moderation. This page explains exactly how the staff team handles rule violations — what punishments exist, how they're applied, and what your rights are if you believe a decision was unfair. Understanding the moderation system helps you know what to expect and how to keep your experience positive.

<Info>
  Moderation logs record block changes, chest access, and chat history for every player. These logs exist to protect you — they're the primary tool staff use to prove griefing, roll back damage, and clear innocent players of false accusations. Logs are only reviewed in the context of active reports or investigations.
</Info>

## Punishment Tiers

Staff follow a structured escalation system. Each new offense moves a player further up the tier ladder, and all punishments are logged permanently against your account.

| Level | Punishment | Examples |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Verbal Warning | Minor spam, accidental rule violation |
| 2 | Mute (1 hour) | Repeat spam, mild harassment |
| 3 | Mute (24 hours) | Ongoing harassment, hate speech |
| 4 | Temp Ban (3 days) | Griefing, first-time cheating offense |
| 5 | Temp Ban (7 days) | Repeat griefing, confirmed hacked client |
| 6 | Permanent Ban | Doxxing, severe hacking, irreversible griefing |

Severity can be escalated beyond the standard tier progression for particularly serious violations. Staff use their judgment to match the punishment to the offense — a first-time severe violation may jump directly to a higher tier.

## Moderation Principles

Staff are trained to apply punishments fairly and consistently. Every action taken is logged in the moderation system with a reason, timestamp, and the staff member responsible. No punishment is issued silently — you will always receive an in-game or Discord notification explaining what rule was violated and for how long any restriction applies.

All staff decisions are subject to review. If you believe a punishment was applied incorrectly, you have the right to appeal — the only exception is a final ruling made directly by the Owner after a completed appeal process.

## What Mods Can See

Moderators have access to a range of server logs to support their investigations. These tools are used exclusively for rule enforcement and are never used to spy on regular gameplay without cause.

- **Block history** — every block placed or broken is logged with a timestamp and player name
- **Chest access logs** — who opened a container and when
- **Chat logs** — full chat history including private messages where required by a formal investigation

If your land was griefed, a Moderator can use these logs to identify the culprit and roll back any damage using CoreProtect.

## Appealing a Punishment

If you've received a punishment you believe was unfair or issued in error, you can submit an appeal. Head to the [Reporting & Appeals](/community/reporting) page for full instructions on how to open a ticket and what information to include.

Appeals for mutes and temp bans are reviewed by Senior Moderators. Permanent ban appeals are escalated to an Admin or Owner. Provide as much context as possible — screenshots, timelines, and witness names all help your case.

## Staff Code of Conduct

Staff members are held to the same rules as every other player — and then some. Holding a staff role is a privilege, not a license. Staff are expected to be impartial, professional, and never abuse their permissions for personal gain.

If you witness a staff member breaking rules, abusing commands, or behaving inappropriately, report it directly to an Admin or Owner through a private Discord ticket. Do not post staff misconduct reports in public channels. All reports are taken seriously and investigated confidentially.
