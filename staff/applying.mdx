DirtShop SMP runs entirely on volunteer staff — players who genuinely care about the community and want to help make the server better for everyone. If you've spent time helping newcomers, keeping chat friendly, or just being a positive presence on the server, we'd love to hear from you. The Helper role is where every staff journey begins, and there's always room for dedicated people to grow.

## Why We Need Staff

A server is only as good as its community, and the community is only as good as the people looking after it. Helpers and Moderators are the first line of support for players who are confused, upset, or in need of assistance. Without a strong volunteer team, issues go unresolved and the experience suffers for everyone. By joining staff, you directly shape the culture and quality of DirtShop SMP.

## Requirements

Before applying, make sure you meet every item on this list. Applications that don't meet the requirements are closed without review.

- Regular rank or higher (minimum 24 hours playtime)
- Active on the server for at least 2 weeks before applying
- Active in the Discord community
- No active punishments in the last 30 days
- Fluent in English (other languages are a plus)
- Minimum 13 years of age

## What We Look For

Meeting the requirements gets your application read — but the following qualities are what get you hired. You don't need deep technical knowledge of Minecraft plugins or server administration; that can be taught. What can't be taught is character.

- **Maturity** — you handle conflict calmly and don't escalate situations
- **Patience** — you're willing to answer the same question for the tenth new player without frustration
- **Fairness** — you apply rules consistently regardless of who's involved, including friends
- **Reliability** — you show up regularly and follow through on commitments
- **Helpfulness** — you already help players before you're asked to

Technical knowledge of Minecraft game mechanics, commands, or plugin features is genuinely helpful and worth mentioning in your application, but it is not a requirement.

## Application Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm you meet all requirements">
    Double-check every item in the requirements list above. If you're unsure about your playtime, use `/playtime` in-game. If you have a recent punishment, wait until 30 days have passed before applying.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Discord #staff-applications">
    Join the [DirtShop SMP Discord](https://discord.gg/dirtshop) and navigate to the **#staff-applications** channel. Make sure you can read the pinned messages before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the pinned application template">
    There is a pinned message in #staff-applications containing the official template. Read it fully before writing anything — every question has a specific purpose and skipping or skimming the instructions is one of the most common reasons applications are rejected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill out the template and post it">
    Write your responses carefully and honestly. Copy the full template into a new message, fill in each section, and post it in the channel. Do not DM your application to a staff member.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for a response">
    Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. You can expect a response within **up to 2 weeks**. During this time, continue playing normally and being a positive part of the community — staff notice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete an interview if accepted">
    If your application is approved, an Admin will reach out to schedule a short interview over Discord voice or text chat. This is a friendly conversation to get to know you better — not an exam. Be yourself.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for a Good Application

The best applications are honest, specific, and grounded in real experience. Generic answers like "I want to help because I love the server" tell us very little. Instead, give concrete examples:

- Describe a specific time you helped a new player figure something out
- Explain a conflict you witnessed (or were part of) and how you handled or would handle it
- Be upfront about your weaknesses — self-awareness is a green flag
- Write in full sentences; spelling and grammar matter because they reflect how you'll communicate as staff

Padding your application with filler or exaggerating your activity level is easy to verify and immediately disqualifying.

<Warning>
  Do **not** ask staff members about the status of your application. Messaging staff in DMs, asking in public channels, or bringing it up in-game may result in your application being automatically rejected. The review process takes time — you will be contacted when a decision is made.
</Warning>
